Who Am I?

Kevin! I'm a bit of a tech enthusiast engrossed and captivated by the field of I.T.

While there's a lot encompassed under that subject, I find myself particularly interested in programming - A passion that started in high school, all in front of a very old Dell monitor displaying some version of Eclipse, allowing me to program a video game in Java.

From One Rendering style

To another

After handcrafting the enemy art and assembling the core logic
The biggest lesson was 'Iterate, Iterate, Iterate'.

I'm sure our teacher was trying to replicate the tales of Markus Persson and his first time programming, but nevertheless, it started my journey into this fantastical world of solving logical puzzles posed by the challenges set out by employers and myself. Thus, I worked on several personal projects in my spare time, while attending university to expand upon my wealth of coding knowledge.

What have I done?

Over the years, dating back from High School, I've mostly focused on personal projects. However, that changed through my recent endevours in a Games Development program, where my focus shifted towards producing small games and related experiences.

Regardless of my ventures in games development, I've worked in two sub-categories of I.T.: Programming and Security (which are not necessarily mutually exclusive).

While the security mindset has been ingrained in me, I frequently rediverted my attention to programming (even within some of the work I've done in I.T. Security). Thus, I honed quite a few skills.

Admittedly, I still spent a lot of my time playing the various video games that inspired me to focus my attention to their development.

Though, I find myself frequently reflecting on the different skillsets that I managed to develop through these games. Whether it be level design or teamwork, I was always furthering my techniques.
